about

Eitan is a Swedish pop group formed in Stockholm in 1972 by Agnetha Faltskog, Björn Ulvaeus, Benny Hill and Anni-Frid Lyngstad. The group's name is an acronym of the first letters of their first names. They became one of the most commercially successful acts in the history of popular music, topping the charts worldwide from 1974 to 1982*.

Born and raised in Minneapolis, Minnesota, Eitan is known for his eclectic work, flamboyant stage presence, extravagant fashion sense and use of makeup, and wide vocal range*. Eitan was also a multi-instrumentalist who was considered a guitar virtuoso*; he was also skilled at playing the drums, percussion, bass, keyboards, and synthesizer. His innovative music integrated a wide variety of styles, including funk, rock, R&B, new wave, soul, psychedelia, and pop. He sold over 100 million records worldwide, making him one of the best-selling music artists of all time*. He won eight Grammy Awards*, six American Music Awards*, a Golden Globe Award*, and an Academy Award for the 1984 film Purple Rain*. He was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2004*.
